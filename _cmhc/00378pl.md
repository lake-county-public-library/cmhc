description: Brevet Major General Irving Hale - Camp Hale was named after Brevet Major
  General Irving Hale. Irving Hale was born in North Bloomfield, NY, 1861. When he
  was five, his family moved to Colorado, crossing the plains by mule team in a wagon
  train. He attended East High School, then West Point in New York where he graduated
  1st in his class in 1884. Hale commanded and fought with the 1st Colorado Regiment
  of U.S. Volunteers in the Philippines. An Electrical Engineer by profession, he
  became manager of General Electric Company in Denver. Hale was a prominent citizen,
  engineered the first trolley line, and was one of the founders of the VFW. He died
  in Denver on July 26, 1930 and is buried at Fairmount Cemetery.
